# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Job candidates and hiring companies in Angola (and Portugal) using Parvagas as a
recruitment platform. Candidates are often job-searching under real pressure —
they need to get a working profile/CV published and applications moving with
minimal friction, on connections and devices that vary widely. Companies need
to post jobs, review applicants, and manage hiring with a clear, no-nonsense
workflow.

## Product Purpose

Parvagas connects Angolan (and Portuguese) job seekers with employers: a
candidate portal (profile, CV builder, applications, job search) and a company
portal (job postings, applicant review, team/billing). Success is a candidate
who gets from "no CV" to "published, submitted application" with as few
confusing steps as possible, and a recruiter who can review/act on applicants
without hunting for the next step.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, efficient, direct. Confident but not flashy — built for a real
recruitment workflow, not a marketing showcase. Existing brand system: red-600
as the single accent/CTA color, slate neutrals, white surfaces, rounded-xl/2xl
cards, dark slate-950 asides for hero/context panels. Voice is plain
Portuguese (pt-PT/pt-AO), no jargon, no hype language.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS/AI-slop defaults: emoji used as icons (⭐ instead of a proper icon
component), uniform identical-card grids repeated without hierarchy,
gradient/star-badge upsell banners, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every
section, side-stripe colored borders as the default "accent" move. If a
pattern could have been generated by any AI tool for any SaaS product without
customization, it doesn't belong here.

## Design Principles

- Serve the task, don't decorate it — every candidate-portal screen exists to
  move someone through a real workflow step (upload CV, review data, submit
  application); visual flourish that doesn't support that is noise.
- One accent, used deliberately — red-600 is the brand's single strong color;
  don't introduce competing accent colors (amber, gradients) for upsells or
  status unless there's no reasonable alternative within the existing palette.
- Real icons, not emoji — Heroicons (already used throughout the app) instead
  of emoji glyphs, for both visual consistency and because emoji render
  inconsistently across platforms.
- Hierarchy over sameness — not every piece of information deserves an
  identical white bordered card; use layout, typography weight, and spacing to
  show what matters most on a screen.
- Plain, honest copy — Portuguese/English copy that describes what a feature
  does, not marketing language layered onto a utility screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target on record; treat body text contrast (≥4.5:1) and
touch-target sizing as a baseline given many candidates are on mobile data
connections and older devices. No specific reduced-motion or colorblind
accommodations requested yet — apply standard `prefers-reduced-motion`
support for any new motion.
